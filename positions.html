Positions

What Are the Positions?

The game of soccer has four main positions, they are, from back to front: goalkeeper, defender, midfielder and striker. Players on each team are assigned a position; this tells them what their role in the game is. Each main position can be broken down into sub-positions, with the exception of goalkeeper, to give further information on the player in that positions role.

On the left, you will see an example of a player layout with yellow as the goalkeeper, blue as the defenders, purple as midfielders and red as strikers. It shows what we call a 4-4-2 layout, meaning there is one goalkeeper, four defenders, four midfielders and two strikers. You may notice it is not 1-4-4-2, the 1 being for the goalkeeper, this is because there cannot be any more or less than one goalkeeper on each team.

So, what does everyone do, then?

Striker

Also known as the attacker, a striker’s objective is to score goals and harry the opposition’s defenders. Strikers are often exceptionally quick and can make shots from a distance.

Prospective strikers should be sure to familiarise themselves with the offside rule and how penalty and free kicks are taken.
